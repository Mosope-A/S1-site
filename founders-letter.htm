FOUNDER’S LETTER


My Journey into
Digital Assets

OCT 10 2025

Below, through the window behind me at BitGo’s offices on Wall Street, you can see the New York Stock Exchange, with Fearless Girl just around the corner. And as I write this letter to the current and future shareholders of BitGo, I can’t help but wonder: how did I get here?

I certainly never wanted to work on Wall Street. While many of my college classmates went to work in finance, my path was different. I was interested in systems, networking, and scalability - I wanted to build products. It wasn’t until 2012 that I would learn of Bitcoin, and the two worlds collided for me. Like others, Bitcoin inspired me to dig into money - what makes good money, how do our markets work, and can we build a better financial system? Suddenly it was clear - financial markets were like the Galápagos Islands - prehistoric and basically untouched by modern technology. It was time for a change.

I started my career just as the Web was beginning to unfold, and I joined Netscape before it was a household name. There, I witnessed the growth of the Internet Bubble and eventually the dot-com crash. Some mock that period as a “peak bubble” but that’s not what it was. That was the natural process of markets sorting out winners and losers after a major technology disruptor. And that process sorted out some big winners - like Amazon and Google.

Today, we sit 15 years after another major technology disruptor: Bitcoin and blockchains. This time we’re revolutionizing the global financial system. Some call that “Web 3.” But this revolution is harder than Web 1.0 because it deals with money. And when it comes to money, we at BitGo always hold ourselves to a higher bar. In addition to technology, this disruption changes legislation, regulation, and power in all countries. We’re currently in the middle of that same natural process that followed Web 1.0, and the market is shaking out which businesses are valuable and which are not. Many will fail.

From a traditional finance perspective, many criticize the digital asset industry as “re- learning the history of Wall Street.” But we’re not re-learning; we’re re-architecting. Unlike the traditional financial system and related regulations that predated digital assets, digital asset builders today have the advantage of a pretty simple element: computers. The architects of the new financial system will not be financiers looking to make a quick buck, they will be experts in fault-tolerance, distributed systems, rules engines, and algorithms. When we’re done, global markets won’t be subject to the control of a few large financial institutions, but will instead be protected by the immutable mathematics of the many.

The Digital Asset
Infrastructure Company


In the way that AWS was a pioneer among internet infrastructure companies, BitGo is a pioneer among digital asset infrastructure companies. We started with a simple idea in 2013: digital assets needed secure storage technology. We pioneered the 2-of-3 multi-signature protocol, now the gold standard for securing Bitcoin. But we didn't stop there

By 2014, we had built a pioneering institutional-grade wallet infrastructure for multi-user wallet with policy managment. By 2015 we were an innovator in launching a normalized API securing multiple blockchains, from Bitcoin, to Ethereum to Litecoin and more. Over time, we expanded to deliver many other innovations, such as the first commercial implementation of 2-of-3 multi-party-computation(MPC). Technology never stops evolving-and neither does BitGo



we didn't stop at wallets. As the ecosystem grew, so did the needs. Exchanges needed reliable custody to back their trading desks. Protocols required staking secure solutions to reward participants without exposing them to slash risks. Token issuers wanted compliant infrastruture to launch Stablecoins and real world assets(RWAs). BitGo swiftly positioned to become the backbone- a foundational layer powering it all. Today, the BitGoo platform is used to protect over $100 billioon in assets, support more than 1400 digital assets, and facilitate billions in trades. We've custodied assets for gorvernments like El salvador and Bhutan, powered liquidity for major exchanges, and enabled fintechs to embed digital assets seamlessly into their apps. We're not just participants; we're architects of the infrastructure that makes the digital asset economy possible.


And let's be clear: this wasn't luck. it was delibrate design. while others chased retail hype or speculative trading, we focused on the unsexy but essential: security, compliance, and scalability. We became one of, if not the first qualified custodians purpose-built for digital assets, earning SOC 1 and SOC 2 attestations, CCSS audits, and offering up to $250 million in insurance coverage for digital assets held in qualified custody. In a space riddled with scandals,BitGo has remained steady,safe, and growing. That's not arrogance, that's track record forged in the fires of multiple crypto winters.

Financial Services
Infrastructure


"Infrastructure" usually connotes "technology" But,when it comes to finance, infrastructure is more than just technology.It's also the regulatory license to operate that technology,and the compliance to monitor an protect our clients.


The technology layer of BitGo allows us to operate with our clients in over 100 countries today. Clients can leverage our platform utilizing their own regulatory licenses to deliver financial services products all over the world. In Japan, for example, many of the licensed crypto exchanges utilize BitGo’s self-managed cold-storage to meet the stringent requirements of their regulators.

Today, most of our clients leverage not only BitGo’s technology, but also our financial services capabilities. And this is where we see the most opportunity for growth. Rather than needing to interface with countless regulators, staking providers, liquidity providers, exchanges, and financiers, clients are opting to use BitGo’s solutions. We do the heavy lifting of building the technology, global connectivity, and compliance, which allows our clients to focus on managing their customers, their distribution channels, and their applications.


We believe that BitGo’s services are the strongest and most robust in the industry:

  • A full-service self-custody wallet platform
  • Regulated custody in multiple jurisdictions around the world
  • Staking with broad partnerships with all major validators
  • Liquidity with access to all major global exchanges
  • Fully collateralized borrowing & lending
  • Settlement services
  • Stablecoin access and conversion

BitGo has always sought the regulatory licenses for the activities we undertake in advance of entering into those activities. As such, we interface with dozens of regulators across our global footprint, with major regulated operations in the US, Europe, the Middle East, and Singapore. Few companies have this global footprint, and this is what powers the Go Network, giving our clients the ability to settle trades across the globe. Our clients want to know that we are not only operating in a compliant manner for them, but that we also do so for all of our clients everywhere. We’re trusted to do it right. Always.

Transparency as
a public company


Since our founding, BitGo has been nothing if not transparent. All of our roles — as a software provider, regulated custodian, and financial services provider — require us to be completely open about how our technology and processes work along with audits, attestations, and more. And in this angle, we, our clients, our regulators and our auditors are all aligned: transparency is key.

As such, the transition of BitGo into the public markets is simply the next evolution of this transparency. Digital assets have now taken the center stage in mainstream, traditional finance. Most of these traditional finance companies are public, have been so for decades and meet the highest standards and the highest bar for their clients. They want BitGo to be a public company as they build their own products and rely on BitGo’s infrastructure. As such, this transition will help us expand our business and differentiate ourselves from our competitors.

The Future is Bright


Redefining the future of money takes hard work and coordination of many parties.We need more standardization, more agreement, and more infrastructure. Fortunately, BitGo is at the center of it.

We believe that everything will be a digital asset. Today’s digital asset market is a mere $4 trillion. As RWAs take foot, we believe that trillions more will move into the digital realm. BitGo’s job is to enable and accelerate this transition. Along the way, we hope to leave a mark, making digital assets usable for all.

I can’t conclude this letter without a major thank you — to all the employees at BitGo that work tirelessly on this vision, to the investors that saw the vision from our early days, to the clients that had the patience to work through the kinks as we built this vision, and finally to the investors yet to come who share our vision of an open, global, digital financial system. We look forward to the exciting future with you!






Mike Belshe
Co-Founder & CEO, BitGo
